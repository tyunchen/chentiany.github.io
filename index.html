    Research Experience¶

    Project 1: Isoflurane anesthesia regulates transcription of RORα and Rev-erbα genes and their circadian rhythm changes in the hippocampus and cerebral cortex of mice¶

    Participant (Instructed by) Nanjing, China, 2021

    • Research Objective: To investigate the effects of isoflurane anesthesia on the transcription levels of clock genes RORα and Rev-erbα and their circadian rhythm changes in the hippocampus and cerebral cortex of mice.
    • Research Outcome:
      • RORα mRNA expression is upregulated in the hippocampus and downregulated in the cerebral cortex.
      • Rev-erbα mRNA expression decreased in the cerebral cortex.
      • The circadian rhythm of RORα and Rev-erbα mRNA expressions in the hippocampus and cerebral cortex shifted significantly upon isoflurane anesthesia administration.
      • Summary: Isoflurane anesthesia can induce the changes of RORα andRev-erbα transcription levels and circadian rhythm changes in the hippocampus and cerebral cortex of mice.

    Project 2: Effect of melatonin pretreatment on TRF1 protein in human neuroblastoma cells treated with isoflurane¶

    Participant (Instructed by) Nanjing, China, 2022

    • Research Objective: To observe the effect of melatonin pretreatment on TRF1 in isoflurane-treated human neuroblastoma cells (SHSY-5Y).
    • Research Outcome:
      • The protein levels of p-Akt (Ser473) and TRF1 in SH-SY5Y cells treated with 100 nmol/L concentration of melatonin and 1.5% isoflurane were significantly increased.
      • The PI3K (p110α) selective inhibitor BYL719 can inhibit the increase of p-Akt and TRF1 caused by melatonin in SH-SY5Y cells.
      • Summary: Melatonin pretreatment significantly up-regulates the expression of TRF1 protein in SHSY-5Y cells after isoflurane treatment, whose mechanism may be related to PI3K/Akt signaling pathway.
